# Parachute — 2-Minute Pitch

*~290 words. Target: 2 minutes at natural pace.*
*Bracketed notes are stage directions, not spoken.*

---

Everyone is racing to build your AI agent. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, a hundred startups — over 100 million people already pay $20 a month for AI. It's a $50 billion market growing at 44% a year.

But features get cloned in weeks. It's a race to the bottom. So we stopped trying to win it.

Here's what we noticed: every one of these platforms has memory now — and it's all the same. One flat text file. It remembers your name, not six months of your thinking. And the way you get your thinking *into* the system is broken — you have to have a conversation. The AI is always in the middle. There's no way to just think for yourself and have it become context.

**Parachute is a memory layer for humans and AI.** Our first product is Parachute Daily — a voice-first journal. You speak into a wearable pendant or your phone, on a walk, in the car, wherever thinking happens. Your thoughts become structured knowledge — not locked inside one platform.

*[Hold up pendant if available]*

What makes this work: we're built on MCP, the open standard for connecting AI to tools. Whatever AI you already use — Claude, ChatGPT, anything — you don't switch. You *add* Parachute. Your context compounds over months and years in a real graph database. It's open source, it's yours, and it's portable. We're not trying to lock anyone in — we're building something so simple and useful that people *choose* to stay.

We're self-funded. Working app, working MCP server, pendant prototype — you can try it today. Beta launching this month. Five-person founding team across software, hardware, and design.

We're raising $300K on a SAFE to take the team full-time and launch by June. Revenue starts immediately.
